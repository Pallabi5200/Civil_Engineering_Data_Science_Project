# Document Understanding
### PMPL documents

==================================================================

## Document 1 – Work Order


Purpose

The Work Order is the official authorization issued by the client to the contractor to begin the project. It defines the project scope, commercial terms, timeline, payment milestones, contractual obligations, and project conditions. It is the first document in the project lifecycle.

Who Creates It?

Client

Who Receives It?

Contractor

When Is It Used?

Before project execution begins.

Why Is It Important?

* Starts the project officially.
* Defines legal obligations.
* Specifies project duration.
* Defines payment milestones.
* Establishes penalties for delays.

==================================================================

## Document 2 – BOQ

Purpose

The Bill of Quantities (BOQ) is the operational planning document of the project. It describes every engineering activity required to complete the work along with quantities and units.

Who Creates It?

Engineering Team / Estimation Team

Who Uses It?

Project Manager

Procurement Team

Site Engineer

Finance Team

Why Is It Important?

* Cost estimation
* Material planning
* Manpower planning
* Billing
* Procurement
* Project execution

==================================================================

## Document 3 – Purchase Order

Purpose

The Purchase Order is an official procurement document issued by the contractor to suppliers for purchasing materials required for project execution.

Who Creates It?

Procurement Team

Who Receives It?

Material Supplier

Why Is It Important?

* Material procurement
* Cost control
* Supplier management
* Delivery planning
* Inventory planning

# Document 4 – Proforma Invoice (PI)

## What is a Proforma Invoice?

A Proforma Invoice (PI) is a preliminary commercial invoice issued by the supplier before the actual sale takes place. It is not a tax invoice and is not used for accounting or tax purposes. Instead, it serves as a formal request for advance payment based on the agreed Purchase Order.

In this project, DYWIDAG Bridgecon India Pvt. Ltd. issued the Proforma Invoice to Pallabi Infracon Pvt. Ltd. for the supply of GEWI Bars and related accessories after receiving the Purchase Order. The invoice refers to Purchase Order No. PIPL/DSI/4/25-26 dated 08/10/2025 and includes the agreed quantity (54 units), pricing, GST, and payment terms.

---

## Why is a Proforma Invoice Required?

The supplier issues the Proforma Invoice to:

* Confirm the agreed commercial terms.
* Request the advance payment.
* Confirm material quantity and pricing.
* Initiate material manufacturing or dispatch.
* Allow the buyer to arrange funds before shipment.

---

## Who Creates It?

Supplier

DYWIDAG Bridgecon India Pvt. Ltd.

---

## Who Receives It?

Contractor

Pallabi Infracon Pvt. Ltd.

---

## When Is It Used?

After the Purchase Order has been accepted but before the actual Tax Invoice is generated and before material dispatch.

---

## Why is it Important?

* Starts the financial transaction.
* Confirms commercial agreement.
* Initiates supplier payment.
* Supports procurement planning.
* Acts as the bridge between procurement and accounting.

---

## Key Takeaways

* The Proforma Invoice is not the final invoice.
* It requests advance payment before dispatch.
* It confirms the commercial details agreed in the Purchase Order.
* It begins the project's financial transaction between supplier and contractor.

Document 5 – Tax Invoice

What is a Tax Invoice?

A Tax Invoice is the official commercial and legal invoice issued after goods are supplied or services are performed. Unlike a Proforma Invoice, a Tax Invoice is an accounting document that records an actual business transaction and is used for GST compliance.

In the PMPL project, Pallabi Infracon Pvt. Ltd. issues Tax Invoices to Maharaja Shree Umaid Mills Ltd. after completing specific contractual milestones such as:

• 40% Advance Stage
• 25% After Chipping, Drilling and Grouting
• 20% After Steel Work and Concreting
• 10% After Project Completion and Submission of Tax Invoice

The invoices include:

• Invoice Number
• Invoice Date
• Client Details
• Project Details
• Description of Work
• Quantity
• GST
• Gross Amount
• Net Payable Amount

What is a Handing Over Record?

A Handing Over Record is the final technical quality documentation submitted by the contractor after completing the construction work. It provides evidence that every engineering activity has been completed according to project specifications and quality standards before the project is formally handed over to the client.

Unlike invoices, this document is not primarily financial—it is a quality assurance and project completion document.

Document 7 – Test Certificates

What is a Test Certificate?

A Test Certificate is an official quality assurance document issued by the material manufacturer or an authorized testing laboratory. It confirms that construction materials or completed construction work have been tested according to relevant engineering standards and meet the required specifications.

Unlike a Proforma Invoice, a Test Certificate is not a commercial document. It is a technical quality document.

------------------------------------------------------------

Why is it Issued?

• To verify material quality before use.
• To demonstrate compliance with engineering standards.
• To confirm that completed work meets structural requirements.
• To support project quality audits.
• To provide traceability of construction materials.
• To satisfy client and consultant quality requirements.

------------------------------------------------------------

Who Creates It?

Material Manufacturer
OR

Independent Testing Laboratory

Examples found in the PMPL project:

• Fosroc Chemicals
• Sika India
• Yashodhan Control Lab
• Disan RMC Laboratory

------------------------------------------------------------

Before material approval

During construction

During quality inspection

Before project handover

• Ultrasonic Pulse Velocity Test

What is a Work Completion Certificate (WCC)?

A Work Completion Certificate (WCC) is the official document issued after the contractor has completed the assigned work and the client (or client's representative) verifies that the work has been executed according to the approved scope.

Unlike the Purchase Order, which authorizes work, or the Tax Invoice, which requests payment, the WCC confirms that the work has actually been completed and accepted.

Why is a WCC issued?

The WCC serves several important purposes:

Confirms the repair work has been completed.
Confirms the work matches the Purchase Order.
Records the completion date.
Verifies that the client has inspected the work.
Acts as supporting evidence before payment is released.

Without a WCC, the contractor generally cannot justify the final billing.


Who prepares the WCC?

The contractor prepares the certificate after completing the work.

It is then reviewed and acknowledged by the client's representatives, such as the Site Engineer or Site In-charge.

PERFORMA INVOICE


What is a Proforma Invoice?

A Proforma Invoice is a preliminary commercial invoice issued before the final Tax Invoice.

Unlike a Tax Invoice, a Proforma Invoice does not record the final sale. Instead, it informs the client about:

the work completed up to a billing milestone,
the amount that will be billed,
applicable taxes,
payment due,
and the Purchase Order against which the claim is made.

Think of it as a payment request based on project progress rather than a completed financial transaction.

Why is a Proforma Invoice issued?

Construction projects usually last several weeks or months.

If the contractor waits until the entire project finishes, there could be significant cash-flow challenges.

Instead, milestone billing is used.

For example:

Purchase Order Issued
        │
        ▼
Project Starts
        │
        ▼
40% Work Completed
        │
        ▼
Proforma Invoice Raised
        │
        ▼
Client Reviews
        │
        ▼
Payment Process
        │
        ▼
Continue Construction

This allows the contractor to receive partial payments while the project is still underway.

What is a Tax Invoice?

A Tax Invoice is the final commercial document issued after the contractor completes an approved stage of work or completes the entire work as per the Purchase Order.

Unlike a Proforma Invoice, which is only a payment request, the Tax Invoice is the official accounting document used for payment processing and GST compliance.

It becomes part of both the contractor's and the client's financial records.

Why is a Tax Invoice issued?

The Tax Invoice serves multiple purposes:

Requests payment for completed work.
Records GST liability.
Confirms the value of work executed.
Links completed work with the Purchase Order.
Provides legal proof of the commercial transaction.

Without a Tax Invoice, payment cannot normally be processed.


Who prepares the Tax Invoice?

The contractor:

Pallabi Infracon Private Limited

Who receives it?

The client company responsible for the project, such as:

depending on the project site

What is a Work Completion Certificate (WCC)?

A Work Completion Certificate (WCC) is an official document issued after the client verifies that the contractor has completed the work according to the Purchase Order, Scope of Work, BOQ, and quality requirements.

It confirms that:

Construction work has been completed.
The client has inspected the work.
The completed work meets the agreed specifications.
The contractor is eligible to raise the final invoice.

The WCC acts as the technical acceptance document before financial closure.

Why is a WCC issued?

The WCC is issued to:

Confirm physical completion of construction.
Verify that work meets the required quality standards.
Record the official completion date.
Authorize the contractor to proceed with final billing.
Maintain project completion records.

Without a WCC, disputes can arise about whether the contracted work was actually completed.

Who prepares the WCC?

Normally, the contractor prepares the certificate and submits it after completing the work.

The client's site engineer or authorized representative then inspects the work and signs the certificate to confirm acceptance.


Who signs the WCC?

An Engineering Drawing is the official technical document that tells engineers and contractors exactly how a structure should be built.

Unlike the BOQ, which tells how much work is required, or the Purchase Order, which authorizes the work, the engineering drawing specifies:

Exact dimensions
Structural layout
Foundation details
Material placement
Construction levels

It serves as the primary technical reference during construction.
