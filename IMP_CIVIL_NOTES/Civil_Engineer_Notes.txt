# Document 1 – Work Order (WO)

## What is a Work Order?

A Work Order (WO) is an official legal document issued by the client to the contractor authorizing the contractor to begin the project. It defines the project scope, contract value, payment terms, timeline, responsibilities, and conditions under which the work must be executed.

---

## Why is a Work Order Required?

Without a Work Order, the contractor has no official permission to start the project.

It protects both the client and the contractor by clearly defining:

* Scope of work
* Contract value
* Project duration
* Payment milestones
* Quality requirements
* Penalty clauses
* Warranty and retention terms

Document_2 Grout Application Guide

What is Grouting?

Grouting is the process of filling the gap between the wind turbine steel tower and its concrete foundation with a high-strength, non-shrink grout.

Its purpose is to:

Transfer the turbine load safely to the foundation.
Eliminate voids.
Improve structural stability.
Prevent movement and vibration.

Without proper grouting, the foundation can develop cracks, lose strength, and shorten the turbine's service life.

Key engineering concepts
Surface cleaning before grouting.
Proper storage of grout bags.
Controlled mixing ratio.
Temperature-controlled application.
Continuous pouring to avoid air pockets.
Moist curing.
Compression strength testing before acceptance.

These procedures directly affect repair quality.

Important Engineering Activities
Earthwork Excavation

Soil surrounding the pedestal is excavated to expose the damaged portion of the foundation.


Drilling

18 mm diameter holes are drilled into the concrete using rotary hammer drills.

These holes become injection points for epoxy grout.

PVC Nozzle Installation

PVC nozzles are inserted into drilled holes.

These act as injection ports through which epoxy grout is pumped into cracks and voids.


Pressure Grouting

Low-viscosity epoxy grout is injected under controlled pressure to fill internal cracks and strengthen the concrete foundation.

The documents specify the use of Conbextra EP10 Epoxy Grout, with additional quantities payable if consumption exceeds the assumed amount.


Waterproofing

After grouting, multiple waterproofing coats are applied using specified repair materials to prevent future water ingress and deterioration.

extends the service life of the wind turbine foundation.

Wind Mill Grout Crack Repair Methodology

The methodology document shows the complete repair SOP.

Step 1

Inspect and identify existing hairline cracks.

Step 2

Cut a V-groove along the crack using a disc cutter.

Step 3

Seal the groove using Nitomortar S epoxy mortar.

Step 4

Inject Conbextra EP10 low-viscosity epoxy resin into the prepared crack.

Step 5

Grind the repaired surface after curing to obtain a smooth finish.

Step 6

Repeat the same procedure for all identified cracks.

This is the technical workflow followed on site before completion.

What is a Floor Plan?

A Floor Plan is the top view of the building showing:

Room size
Wall positions
Doors
Openings
Internal layout

It is the starting point for site marking.

What is a Roof Plan?

A Roof Plan shows:

Roof dimensions
Roof sheet arrangement
Roof slope
Drainage direction

This ensures proper rainwater runoff.

What is a Foundation Plan?

The foundation transfers the building load safely into the soil.

The drawings show isolated footings supporting individual steel columns, with dimensions and spacing clearly defined.

What is an Isolated Footing?

An isolated footing is a reinforced concrete base constructed beneath a single column.

Its purpose is to:

distribute the structural load,
prevent excessive settlement,
improve stability.



What is FGL?

Finished Ground Level

The final ground surface after filling and leveling.

What is FFL?

Finished Floor Level

The completed floor level after flooring work.

What is Elevation (EL)?

Elevation indicates the vertical height of different construction components relative to a reference level.

Why are Anchor Bolts important?

Anchor bolts connect steel columns to the concrete foundation.

They resist:

wind loads,
uplift,
horizontal forces.

Why is Plinth Protection provided?

Plinth protection prevents:

water seepage,
soil erosion,
weakening of the foundation.
Why is Rainwater Pipe shown?

The drawings include rainwater pipes to safely drain water from the roof and prevent damage to the structure.

Civil Engineering Learning

Engineering drawings convert design into construction instructions. Every activity on site—from excavation to steel erection and roofing—is guided by these drawings.
